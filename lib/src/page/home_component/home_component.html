Quick Start

In Prepare the Robots six robots are randomly prepared for competition. Use them 'as is' or click on the blue bar to instantly adjust their capabilities. Then go to Power Up! and start the match. Watch them scurry around to fulfill the their default strategies, and see the points stack up.

Take Control

You can drive robot 'Red 1' simply by toggling 'I want to drive this robot' in Prepare the Robots . Then you are in control. Click on a cube source to grab a cube, or click on a cube target to score points.

A fun way to start is to make your robot (Red 1) a super robot, and make all the others not so good.

Don't worry if your robot occaisionally takes an amusing detour on its way to a destination. The simulator calculates how long the robot should take, and the animation is tuned to take that long.

Tweak the Robot

Click on 'More Configuration Options' to reveal 2 more blue bars to specify the robot's speed of movement and agility at manipulating cubes.

'Performance Details' shows the editable performance specs. Notice that changing any of the blue sliders affects these values instantly. You can observe your team's actual robot and enter the values here to get an idea of how it might perform in competition. Disclaimer: The perfomance range from 'Basic Bot' to 'Super Bot' is arbitrary and may not reflect actual robot performance.

In 'Performance Details' you'll see entries for '% Variation' and '% Failure'. These numbers are used to calculate semi-random robot behaviors. [Note: '% Failure' is not yet implemented]

Tweak Strategies

This is how you instruct the robot what to do when by specfying goals (for example, "my switch" or "scale" or "climber"). You can configure each goal:

  1. Start At -- Enter the number of seconds when the task becomes available. Auton starts 0, teleop starts at 15, and endgame starts at 120
  2. End At -- Enter the number of seconds when the task is no longer available.
  3. Priority -- 10 indicates a higher priority than 1. Priority is used to choose between available tasks when all other features are equivalent.
  4. Maintain Power Cube Lead Margin -- This rule applies to switches and the scale. It can be used to express, 'Try to keep the specified number of cubes ahead of the opposition' .
  5. Maximum Power Cube Count -- Ignore this task if the power cube target already has the specified number of cubes.

Save and Share Your Favorites

If you invest time in tweaking robots and strategies, you'll probably want to save them for reuse later. Your robot and strategy designs can be saved to your browser's datastore. This makes your work available when you reload thos page later. However, it is possible that your work can disappear if your browser resources are cleaned up.

Buttons are provided to manually copy and paste your designs. To paste, click on the web page and press the usual paste short cut keys.

Buttons are also provided to email your designs to yourself or the friends. The human readable data format that you see in the email is called JSON.

Power Up!

As cubes are placed in the vault, you'll notice buttons appear to levitate, force or boost. Pressing the button will exercise the option.
